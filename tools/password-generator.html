Why Use Strong Passwords?

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. A secure password should be long, random, and include a mix of character types to resist brute-force attacks and dictionary attacks.

Password Security Best Practices

  • Use at least 12-16 characters for important accounts
  • Include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Never reuse passwords across different accounts
  • Use a password manager to store passwords securely
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) when available
  • Change passwords if you suspect a breach

How Our Generator Works

Our password generator uses the Web Crypto API (crypto.getRandomValues()) to generate cryptographically secure random passwords. All generation happens in your browser - passwords are never sent to any server.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the generated passwords truly random?

Yes! We use the browser's built-in cryptographic random number generator, which produces high-quality random values suitable for security purposes.

How long should my password be?

We recommend at least 12-16 characters for most accounts. For highly sensitive accounts (banking, email), consider 20+ characters.

Should I include symbols?

Yes! Including symbols significantly increases password strength and makes it harder to crack.
